## 1. PURPOSE AND BACKGROUND

1.1 The Municipality wishes to pilot a citizen digital services and payment reconciliation platform ("the Platform") developed by the Service Provider, on a limited, no-cost, risk-controlled basis ("the Pilot").

1.2 The Pilot is intended to demonstrate the Platform's value in improving service request handling, payment processing, and audit-trail compliance, without obligating either Party to a long-term commercial arrangement.

## 2. SCOPE OF THE PILOT

2.1 The Pilot shall cover the following modules: **[e.g. fault/service request logging; account balance view; sample/anonymised billing data reconciliation]**.

2.2 The Pilot shall run for a period of **[e.g. 60 days]** from the Effective Date, which may be extended by mutual written agreement.

2.3 During the Pilot, the Service Provider shall use **[anonymised / sample / live]** data as agreed in writing in Annexure A.

2.4 No live payment processing or transfer of municipal funds shall occur during the Pilot unless explicitly agreed in writing by both Parties in an addendum.

## 3. NO COST, NO OBLIGATION

3.1 The Pilot is offered at no cost to the Municipality.

3.2 Nothing in this Agreement obligates the Municipality to procure any product or service from the Service Provider following the Pilot, nor does it obligate the Service Provider to continue providing services beyond the Pilot period.

3.3 Any future commercial engagement shall be subject to a separate written agreement and, where applicable, the Municipality's supply chain management policy and the Municipal Finance Management Act 56 of 2003 ("MFMA").

## 4. DATA PROTECTION

4.1 The Parties shall comply with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("POPIA") in respect of any personal information processed during the Pilot.

4.2 Where the Municipality provides data to the Service Provider, the Municipality shall remain the "responsible party" and the Service Provider shall act as "operator" as those terms are defined in POPIA, processing data solely for the Purpose of the Pilot and in accordance with the Municipality's written instructions.

4.3 The Service Provider shall implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to secure any data received and shall not retain, use, or disclose such data for any purpose beyond the Pilot without the Municipality's prior written consent.

4.4 Upon conclusion or termination of the Pilot, the Service Provider shall, at the Municipality's election, return or securely delete all data provided by the Municipality, save to the extent retention is required by law.

## 5. ORIGINATION AND ATTRIBUTION

5.1 The Parties acknowledge that this Pilot, and the underlying concept and platform, was proposed and developed by the Service Provider, and that the Municipality's participation in the Pilot does not transfer any ownership of the Service Provider's intellectual property, methodology, or technology to the Municipality.

5.2 Should the Municipality wish to develop a similar platform internally or through a third party following the Pilot, the Municipality agrees to first afford the Service Provider a right of first refusal to propose a commercial solution, for a period of twelve (12) months following the conclusion of the Pilot.

## 6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 All intellectual property in the Platform, including but not limited to source code, architecture, integration methods, and documentation, remains the sole property of the Service Provider.

6.2 No license is granted to the Municipality beyond the right to use the Platform for the purposes of the Pilot during the Pilot period.

## 7. CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 Each Party shall treat as confidential any non-public information disclosed by the other Party in connection with the Pilot, in accordance with the terms of the Non-Disclosure Agreement signed by the Parties on **[DATE]**, which is incorporated herein by reference.

## 8. LIABILITY

8.1 The Platform is provided on an "as is" basis during the Pilot. The Service Provider shall not be liable for any indirect, consequential, or incidental loss arising from the Municipality's use of the Platform during the Pilot, save in the case of gross negligence or wilful misconduct.

## 9. TERMINATION

9.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement on **[e.g. 7 days']** written notice to the other Party, without penalty.

## 10. GOVERNING LAW

10.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa, and the Parties submit to the jurisdiction of the South African courts.

## 11. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

11.1 This Agreement, together with its Annexures and the incorporated Non-Disclosure Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties relating to the Pilot.
