# Clawback — Growth & TikTok playbook

The product is built. The bottleneck is distribution, not code. This is the
free-distribution plan: TikTok (primary), Reddit, and SEO. The numbers say the
demand is there — **261M consumer disputes/year, only 55% of entitled flight
passengers ever claim, DoNotPay does ~$54M/yr on this exact behaviour** — so
the job is just putting Clawback in front of people at the moment they're angry
about a charge.

> Note: these scripts use the proven "refund-win" formula. Swap in real,
> truthful stories — never fabricate a payout. Honesty is also legal cover:
> the FTC fined DoNotPay $193k for overstating what its letters do. Show the
> letter and the *process*, not a guaranteed outcome.

---

## The formula (why these videos work)

Every high-performing refund-win clip is the same 5 beats, in ~20–30s:

1. **Hook (0–2s)** — a number or a grievance. "This bank fee was R899 for *nothing*."
2. **Relatable problem (2–6s)** — the helpless feeling. "I called 3 times. Nothing."
3. **The turn (6–12s)** — "So I sent them *this* instead." (show the letter on screen)
4. **The lever (12–18s)** — the secret: "It quotes the actual law they're breaking."
5. **The win + CTA (18–25s)** — "R899 back in 6 days. Link in bio." (show refund SMS)

Keep it phone-shot, face-to-camera or screen-record. Captions burned in
(80% watch muted). One idea per video. Post 2–3×/day for 14 days — volume
beats polish; you're buying lottery tickets on the algorithm.

## 8 ready-to-shoot scripts

### 1. The junk bank fee (best cold-open)
> **On-screen hook:** "My bank charged me R899 for 'admin'."
> "I didn't authorise it, and they wouldn't reverse it. So I stopped *asking*
> and started *demanding*. I sent a letter that quotes the Consumer Protection
> Act — the exact section that says they can't do this — with a 14-day deadline
> and a line saying I'll report them to the ombudsman. Six days later: refunded.
> I used Clawback to write it in 60 seconds. Link in bio."

### 2. The flight nobody claims for
> **Hook:** "Your delayed flight owes you up to €600. 45% of people never claim."
> "If your flight was delayed 3+ hours or cancelled, there's a regulation —
> EU261 — that forces the airline to pay you. They're betting you don't know.
> This letter cites it, names the amount, gives them a deadline. Took me a
> minute to make. Tag someone whose flight got wrecked this summer."

### 3. The subscription that won't die
> **Hook:** "They charged me 3 times *after* I cancelled."
> "Every 'are you sure you want to leave' loop is designed to keep your money.
> So I sent a letter citing the auto-renewal rules and demanded every cent back
> plus written confirmation it's cancelled. Money back, subscription dead.
> Stop fighting the chat bot — send the letter."

### 4. Screen-record demo (shows the product)
> **Hook:** "Watch me write a refund demand in 60 seconds."
> *(Screen-record the app: pick 'faulty product', fill 4 fields, preview)*
> "Pick what happened. Type 4 things. It writes the whole letter — and look,
> it quotes the *actual* consumer law, not just 'please refund me'. That's why
> they take it seriously. Copy, send, done."

### 5. The duet/stitch bait (reactive — highest reach)
> Stitch any "company screwed me over" rant. **Hook:** "Don't beg them. Do this."
> "Replying to this — you don't need to argue with support. You need one letter
> that quotes the law and sets a deadline. Here's exactly what it looks like."
> *(show letter)* — Stitching trending grievances is the fastest way to ride
> someone else's reach.

### 6. The faulty product
> **Hook:** "They told me 'no refunds'. They were lying."
> "'No refunds' is not legal when the thing is broken. The law gives you repair,
> replace, or refund — your choice — within 6 months. I quoted the section,
> set a deadline, mentioned the ombudsman. Replacement arrived in a week."

### 7. The "POV" trend format
> **Hook:** "POV: you stopped letting companies keep your money."
> Fast cuts: angry at laptop → typing → letter on screen → refund notification →
> calm sip of coffee. Trending audio. Text overlay: "the letter that does it →
> link in bio."

### 8. The educational/authority builder
> **Hook:** "3 refunds companies legally owe you but hope you never claim."
> "One: delayed flights — up to €600. Two: charges after you cancel — all of it.
> Three: broken goods within 6 months — full refund, your choice. The trick
> isn't asking nicely, it's quoting the law. Save this."

## Beyond TikTok

**Reddit (warm, high-intent):** answer real posts in r/consumer, r/personalfinance,
r/legaladvice-style subs (read each sub's self-promo rules first — give genuine
help, mention the tool only where allowed/asked). People there are *actively*
fighting a charge right now.

**SEO long-tail (compounding, free):** one thin landing page per high-intent
query, each pre-filling the matching scenario:
- "[airline] refund letter template"
- "[bank] dispute charge letter"
- "letter to cancel [gym/subscription] and get refund"
- "faulty product refund letter South Africa / UK"
These rank because the intent is razor-specific and the page solves it in one click.

**The viral loop:** every unlocked letter ends with "— Sent via Clawback".
Recipients (and the people who win) see the brand. Add a one-tap "share my win"
prompt after a user marks a letter as successful.

## 14-day cadence
- **Days 1–2:** film 10–15 clips in one sitting (batch). Set up TikTok + bio link.
- **Days 3–14:** post 2–3/day. Reply to every comment (drives reach).
- **Daily:** 2–3 genuine Reddit answers. Ship 1 SEO page.
- **Watch:** which hook style converts to clicks → make 3 more of that one.
- **Trigger to go live on payments:** first day you see real click-through, swap
  the demo unlock for the live Stripe key (see README).
